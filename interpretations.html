Interpretations

In 1801, a Siksiká Blackfoot chief named Ackomokki collaborated with a Hudson's Bay Company fur trader named Peter Fidler to sketch a birds-eye view of the upper Missouri River and its adjoining tributaries. For the previous year, Ackomokki and Fidler had developed good relations and negotiated with Hudson's Bay to establish the first trading post in Blackfoot Territory.
